5 Camera Settings Every Portrait Photographer Should Know

May 15, 2026 Elena Vasquez Tips

Whether you're shooting with a DSLR, mirrorless, or even a high-end smartphone, understanding your camera settings is the foundation of great portrait photography. Here are the five settings I rely on every day at Aura Photography.

1. Aperture (f-stop)

Aperture controls depth of field — how much of your image is in focus. For portraits, I typically shoot between f/1.8 and f/2.8 to create beautiful background blur (bokeh) that makes your subject pop.

2. Shutter Speed

A good rule of thumb: keep your shutter speed at least double your focal length. With an 85mm lens, aim for 1/170s or faster to avoid motion blur, especially with active subjects like children.

"The best camera setting is the one that lets you capture the moment without thinking about the camera."

3. ISO

Keep ISO as low as possible for the cleanest images. In good natural light, ISO 100–400 is ideal. Don't be afraid to raise it in low light — a slightly grainy sharp photo beats a blurry clean one.

4. White Balance

Shoot in RAW and set a custom white balance when possible. Auto white balance works in most situations, but manual Kelvin settings give you consistent skin tones across a session.

5. Focus Mode

Use single-point autofocus (AF-S) for static portraits and continuous autofocus (AF-C) for moving subjects. Always focus on the nearest eye for the most engaging portraits.

Master these five settings and you'll have the confidence to create stunning portraits in any lighting condition. Happy shooting!
